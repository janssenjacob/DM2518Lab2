Pitepalt

Pitepalt is a traditional dish from the swedish north made of potatoes.

It is preferably eaten with bacon, lingonberry jam, and loads of Bregott.

Ingridients

  • 1 kg Potatoes
  • 1 dl Barley Flour
  • 2 dl Flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp Salt

Directions

  • Peel and grate the potatoes nicely. Allow to drain properly in a strainer or colander and squeeze out the liquid. Cut the pork into small cubes.
  • Mix the potatoes with the barley and wheat flour and salt, start with a smaller amount of flour and add more if needed. The amount of flour depends on the type of potato used. The dough should be fairly firm. Cut the dough into 12 pieces (for 4 port). Do a recess in each. Add about 1 tablespoon of pork cubes, pinch together and shape into round balls.
  • Boil lightly salted water in a large saucepan. Carefully place in half of the columns (for 4 gates) using a hole spoon and gently stir so that they do not stick to the bottom. Simmer for 45-60 minutes.
  • Record with hollow spoon and hot hold. Prepare the rest. Serve with lingonberry jam and some butter to melt on the palette.
